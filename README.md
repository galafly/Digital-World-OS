# Mathematical Modeling of the Digital Universe: Strings Equipped with Four Built-in Engines (F Light / S Space / D Dark / T Time)

## Core Premise

1. Fundamental carrier: The one-dimensional string (open string / closed string) is the sole elementary unit. Instead of treating F, S, D, T as background fields permeating the entire space, we define them as four intrinsic power generators (engines) carried inside each individual string.

2. Cycle rule of energy flow inside strings: Energy circulates in a closed loop: $F \to S \to D \to T \to F$. Two strongly coupled dual pairs exist: $F \leftrightarrow D$, $S \leftrightarrow T$.

3. Core mathematical tools: The Polyakov action of string worldsheets, coupled with intrinsic 1-form wedge product couplings of the four built-in engines, plus a 4-form global constraint for the closed four-dimensional circulation $\boldsymbol{M}=\boldsymbol{F}\wedge\boldsymbol{S}\wedge\boldsymbol{D}\wedge\boldsymbol{T}$.

4. Modeling objective: All particles, spacetime geometry, and four fundamental interactions are derived entirely from coupled vibrations of the four engines embedded within strings, forming a self-consistent digital universe model.

## Step 1: Define Fundamental Geometry & Symbol System (Model Foundation)

### 1.1 Geometry of the string itself (worldsheet)

The surface traced by a moving string is a 2-dimensional worldsheet manifold $\Sigma$, with worldsheet coordinates $\sigma^\alpha=(\sigma^0=\tau,\sigma^1=\sigma)$:

- $\tau$: Evolution time of the worldsheet; $\sigma$: 1-dimensional parametric coordinate of the string ($0\le\sigma\le L$). For open strings, boundary conditions apply at endpoints $\sigma=0,\sigma=L$; closed strings follow periodicity $\sigma\sim\sigma+L$.

- Induced metric on the worldsheet: $\gamma_{ab}(\sigma,\tau)$; intrinsic string tension: $T_0$.

- Embedding map $X^\mu(\sigma,\tau)$: The position of the string embedded within the 4-dimensional target spacetime of the digital universe.

### 1.2 Four built-in engines intrinsic to each string (internal 1-form power flows, not external background fields)

Along the parametric coordinate $\sigma$ of every single string, four groups of intrinsic 1-form power flows (engine output streams) are defined:

$$

\boldsymbol{F}(\sigma,\tau),\;\boldsymbol{S}(\sigma,\tau),\;\boldsymbol{D}(\sigma,\tau),\;\boldsymbol{T}(\sigma,\tau)

$$

Physical definitions:

- $\boldsymbol{F}$: Light power flow; $\boldsymbol{D}$: Dark power flow (strongly coupled dual pair);

- $\boldsymbol{S}$: Space power flow; $\boldsymbol{T}$: Time power flow (strongly coupled dual pair);

- All four quantities are defined on the string’s own worldsheet as intrinsic degrees of freedom, rather than external fields spread across the whole universe.

- Antisymmetry rule of wedge product: $\boldsymbol{A}\wedge\boldsymbol{B}=-\boldsymbol{B}\wedge\boldsymbol{A}$, corresponding to the orientation of energy circulation.

### 1.3 Coupling strength constants (distinguish strong & weak couplings)

- $k_{FD}$: Strong coupling coefficient between Light and Dark;

- $k_{ST}$: Strong coupling coefficient between Space and Time;

- $k_{FS},k_{SD},k_{DT},k_{TF}$: Weak cross-coupling coefficients among the four engines;

- $k_{global}$: Global circulation coupling constant for the closed 4-form fourfold loop.

## Step 2: Construct the Total Action for a Single String $S_{\rm total}$ (Core Equation of the Model)

We take the standard Polyakov string action as the base framework, then add intrinsic kinetic terms of the four embedded engines, pairwise coupling terms, and a global closed-loop circulation term. The total action is split into four components:

$$

S_{\rm total} = S_{\rm string-base} + S_{\rm engine-kin} + S_{\rm pair-couple} + S_{\rm global-cycle}

$$

### 2.1 Base kinetic term of the bare string (Polyakov foundation: stretching and vibration of the string carrier)

$$

S_{\rm string-base} = -\frac{T_0}{2}\int_\Sigma d^2\sigma\sqrt{-\gamma}\;\gamma^{ab}\partial_a X^\mu \partial_b X_\mu

$$

This term describes stretching, translation, and deformation of the bare string moving inside the digital universe, forming the skeleton carrier.

### 2.2 Intrinsic kinetic term of the four independent engines (self-contained energy of each engine)

Kinetic terms for fluctuations of the four groups of 1-form power flows, representing independent energy consumption from the operation of each separate engine:

$$

S_{\rm engine-kin} = -\frac{1}{4}\int_\Sigma d^2\sigma\sqrt{-\gamma}\;\sum_{\Psi\in\{F,S,D,T\}} \star d\Psi \wedge d\Psi

$$

$d\Psi$: Exterior differential of engine power flow (rate of change of output); $\star$: Hodge star operator on the worldsheet, used to scalarize quantities for integration.

### 2.3 Pairwise coupling terms (strong coupling for dual pairs + weak cross-couplings, bidirectional energy transfer between engines)

Corresponds to bidirectional energy exchange between $F\leftrightarrow D$ and $S\leftrightarrow T$ (strong pairs), plus weak energy flow across all other cross combinations, enabling energy transmission between distinct engines:

$$

\begin{aligned}

S_{\rm pair-couple} &= \int_\Sigma d^2\sigma\sqrt{-\gamma}\;\Big[

k_{FD}\; \boldsymbol{F}\wedge\star\boldsymbol{D}

+ k_{ST}\; \boldsymbol{S}\wedge\star\boldsymbol{T} \\

&\quad + k_{FS}\boldsymbol{F}\wedge\star\boldsymbol{S}

+ k_{SD}\boldsymbol{S}\wedge\star\boldsymbol{D}

+ k_{DT}\boldsymbol{D}\wedge\star\boldsymbol{T}

+ k_{TF}\boldsymbol{T}\wedge\star\boldsymbol{F}

\Big]

\end{aligned}

$$

- $\boldsymbol{F}\wedge\star\boldsymbol{D}$: Bidirectional energy exchange between Light engine and Dark engine;

- $\boldsymbol{S}\wedge\star\boldsymbol{T}$: Bidirectional energy exchange between Space engine and Time engine;

Remaining terms represent weak energy circulation across non-dual engine pairs.

### 2.4 Global closed-loop circulation term (core innovation: full four-stage cyclic flow $F\to S\to D\to T\to F$)

The continuous wedge product of all four intrinsic engines, forming a 4-form that describes the complete closed energy cycle inside one string, incorporated into the action as a global constraint term:

$$

S_{\rm global-cycle} = k_{global}\int_\Sigma d^2\sigma\sqrt{-\gamma}\;\; \boldsymbol{M}\wedge\star\boldsymbol{M},\quad \boldsymbol{M}=\boldsymbol{F}\wedge\boldsymbol{S}\wedge\boldsymbol{D}\wedge\boldsymbol{T}

$$

$\boldsymbol{M}$ denotes the density of full coupled circulation inside a single string; $\boldsymbol{M}\wedge\star\boldsymbol{M}$ contracts to a scalar quantity integrable over the worldsheet.

Physical meaning: Enforces a unidirectional cyclic energy flow among the four embedded engines, naturally explaining spin and periodic rotational vibration of microscopic particles.
